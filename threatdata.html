Scam Trends

Scams are growing faster than ever.

Financial fraud and social engineering attacks have surged dramatically across every region. Here is what the data shows.

▲

Global scam losses hit a record high

The Global Anti-Scam Alliance estimated that scams cost consumers worldwide over $1 trillion in 2023 — more than the GDP of most countries. This represents a 3× increase compared to 2019 figures.

2019$310B
2021$650B
2023$1T+
Source: Global Anti-Scam Alliance, 2023
📷

Impersonation scams dominate losses

In Australia, Scamwatch reported that impersonation scams — where criminals pretend to be banks, government agencies, or tech support — accounted for over 45% of all reported financial losses in 2023, totalling $92 million.

  • Bank impersonation$36.5M
  • Government impersonation$28.1M
  • Tech support scams$15.9M
  • Other impersonation$11.5M
Source: Scamwatch Annual Report 2023
📷

Phishing remains the leading attack vector

According to the NCSC's 2023 Annual Review, phishing was the initial access vector in 84% of all cyber incidents responded to by the UK government. SMS phishing (smishing) rose by 328% between 2020 and 2023.

  • Email phishing84% of incidents
  • SMS phishing (smishing)+328% since 2020
  • Voice phishing (vishing)Fastest growing
Source: NCSC Annual Review 2023
♥

Romance scams target all age groups

The FBI IC3 reported that romance and confidence fraud caused $652 million in losses in 2023 — making it the most financially damaging scam type per victim. The average victim lost $35,000, and 30% of victims were under 40.

Under 4030%
40–5937%
60+33%
Source: FBI IC3 Internet Crime Report 2023
Malware & Viruses

Malware attacks are evolving rapidly.

Modern malware is more targeted, more profitable, and harder to detect than ever. Ransomware in particular has moved from nuisance to national-security concern.

🔒

Ransomware payments surpassed $1 billion

Chainalysis confirmed that ransomware payments to attackers exceeded $1.1 billion in 2023 — more than double the 2022 figure of $567 million. Healthcare and critical infrastructure were the most targeted sectors.

2021$765M
2022$567M
2023$1.1B
Source: Chainalysis Crypto Crime Report 2024
💻

560,000 new malware pieces detected daily

AV-TEST Institute registers approximately 560,000 new malware samples every single day. The total number of malware instances in existence passed 1.3 billion in 2023. Info-stealers designed to harvest passwords and session tokens saw the sharpest rise.

  • Trojans58% of all malware
  • Ransomware+73% YoY
  • Info-stealersFastest growing
  • Adware / PUPsDeclining
Source: AV-TEST Institute, 2023
☕

Supply chain attacks tripled in three years

ENISA's Threat Landscape report found that supply chain attacks — where attackers compromise software vendors to reach downstream customers — tripled between 2020 and 2023. The average breach via a supply chain partner cost $4.76 million, 9.5% more than the average breach overall.

2020Baseline
20222× increase
20233× increase
Source: ENISA Threat Landscape 2023
📱

Mobile malware surging on Android

Kaspersky's mobile threat report detected 33.8 million malicious installs on Android devices in 2023 — a 53% increase from 2022. Spyware designed to track location and read messages without user knowledge grew by 421%.

  • Malicious installs (2022)22.1M
  • Malicious installs (2023)33.8M
  • Spyware growth+421%
  • Banking trojans+196%
Source: Kaspersky Mobile Threat Report 2023
Data Breaches

Personal data has never been at greater risk.

Data breaches expose passwords, financial details, and private communications. The scale has become staggering.

3,200+ Data breaches recorded in the US in 2023 alone — an all-time high (ITRC)
353M People affected by US data breaches in 2023
$4.45M Average cost of a data breach globally in 2023 (IBM)
277 days Average time to identify and contain a breach (IBM)
📋

Most breached sectors in 2023

Healthcare remains the most targeted industry for data breaches for the 13th consecutive year, according to IBM's Cost of a Data Breach Report. Financial services and technology round out the top three.

  • Healthcare$10.9M avg breach cost
  • Financial services$5.9M avg breach cost
  • Technology$4.66M avg breach cost
  • Education$3.65M avg breach cost
Source: IBM Cost of a Data Breach Report 2023
🔐

Credential theft drives most breaches

IBM found that stolen or compromised credentials were the most common initial attack vector, involved in 16% of breaches. Breaches using stolen credentials took an average of 328 days to identify — 85 days longer than any other vector.

  • Stolen credentials16% of breaches
  • Phishing15% of breaches
  • Cloud misconfiguration11% of breaches
  • Known unpatched vulnerabilities6% of breaches
Source: IBM Cost of a Data Breach Report 2023
